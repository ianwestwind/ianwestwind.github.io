Ian Kim

Senior Research Scientist  ·  PhD, Preventive Medicine

Ian Kim is a Senior Research Scientist in the Department of Pediatrics at Stanford Medicine. His work applies dynamical systems theory and artificial neural networks to identify behavioral patterns in individuals with, or at elevated risk for, chronic conditions.

He designs computational models of behavior change and health intervention applications that bring precision medicine and behavioral science together. He holds a PhD in Preventive Medicine from the University of Southern California (Keck School of Medicine) and an MFA in New Media from Penn State. Outside research, Ian is a long-distance runner who participates in endurance events in support of charitable causes.

Research work and applications

Behavioral Trajectory Modeling
A computational framework using dynamical systems and deep neural networks to model individual health trajectories for patients managing chronic conditions, enabling personalized intervention timing.
Precision Health Intervention Platform
An AI-assisted platform that synthesizes multimodal clinical and behavioral data to recommend context-specific interventions for chronic disease management across diverse patient populations.
Multimodal Data Infrastructure
Scalable data science infrastructure designed to integrate high-dimensional, multi-timescale clinical datasets, enabling frontier machine learning pipelines for behavioral health research.
